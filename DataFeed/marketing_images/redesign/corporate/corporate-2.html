Company History

In 1981, Jerry and Ron Azarkman opened La Curacao, a consumer product sales enterprise specializing in door-to-door sales, in Burbank, California. During the time of door-to-door sales, La Curacao became popular among the large population of Hispanic immigrants in Los Angeles who were eager to join the consumer market but did not have the financial services available to them. While selling door-to-door, salesmen allowed customers to buy their goods by placing a down payment and then coming back to the customer's residence to collect payments on a regular basis. Customers would agree to payment terms that were affordable based on their word and a hand shake. This eventually evolved into Curacao's credit card service.

In 1983, La Curacao relocated to the Pico-Union district in Los Angeles. By 1984, La Curacao opened its Export division, allowing customers to shop for goods and have them shipped to their families in Mexico and Central America. Throughout the 1980s, La Curacao expanded its array of merchandise, keeping up with the demand for new and hi-tech products. By 1989, the La Curacao department store offered a wide variety of consumer electronics, furniture, toys and cosmetic products. During the Los Angeles Riots in 1992, La Curacao's only location was burnt down and its inventory was completely lost. Two weeks later, the store reopened near its previous location.

In 1995, La Curacao opened its second store in the Panorama City district of the San Fernando Valley, a region in Los Angeles. Located inside the Panorama City Mall, the Panorama store was the first store to feature Mesoamerican style decor and a child care facility for customers' kids. The company also purchased the office towers located on 1605 W. Olympic Boulevard, now the La Curacao Business Center, where the Company's headquarters is currently located. Following the lead of the Panorama store, new La Curacao locations continued the use of Mayan and Aztec themes.

By 2007, the Curacao stores had ten brick-and-morter locations throughout the Southwestern region of the United States with nine stores in California and one in Arizona.

In 2012, La Curacao underwent a rebranding process, renaming themselves Curacao, and introducing a new company logo.
